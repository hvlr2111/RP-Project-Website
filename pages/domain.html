Research Domain

Exploring the theoretical foundations, identified gaps, and the methodological approach of the DTIF architecture.

📚

Literature Survey

The literature review highlights a critical problem: A Fragmented Ecosystem. Despite a significant post-pandemic resurgence, with Sri Lanka reaching 2.36 million arrivals in 2025, the underlying tourism planning infrastructure remains largely based on guesswork and intuition.

The existing body of research demonstrates that crucial destination management aspects—such as macro-level demand forecasting, dynamic visitor flow distribution across hotspots, and real-time sentiment analysis—are currently treated as isolated, siloed tasks. Furthermore, traditional statistical approaches (like ARIMA) frequently fail to capture the shock-sensitive nature of modern tourism demand. In the realm of traveler experience, most existing systems are predominantly English-centric, ignoring local linguistic nuances, and fail to synthesize visual aesthetics with text-based reviews.

The Core Problem

Sri Lanka's tourism sector lacks a cohesive, data-driven intelligence framework to synchronize macro-level demand forecasting with micro-level visitor flow and experiential management. This leads to unmanaged congestion, misinterpretation of traveler sentiments, and generalized service offerings.

Identified Research Gaps

📉 Forecasting Inflexibility

Statistical models like ARIMA fail to capture the non-linear, "shock-sensitive" nature of tourism demand in Sri Lanka.

🗺️ Siloed Flow Management

Traditional flow studies focus on attractions independently, failing to integrate macro-level forecasting with dynamic congestion management.

🌐 Multilingual & Multimodal Gaps

Most recommendation systems are English-centric and ignore local languages like Sinhala and Tamil, failing to integrate visual aesthetics.

🎭 The Sarcasm Barrier

Standard sentiment analysis often misinterprets sarcastic reviews where positive language masks a negative experience.

Research Objectives

To design, develop, and evaluate the Digital Tourism Intelligence Framework (DTIF)—a unified AI architecture aimed at transitioning Sri Lanka's tourism planning to data-driven precision by:

  • Predictive Accuracy: Developing a highly reliable arrival forecasting mechanism using hybrid ensembles.
  • Capacity Control: Creating a dynamic flow distribution model to project and manage visitor congestion across key sites.
  • Personalization: Engineering a multimodal recommendation engine accommodating linguistic nuances and visual aesthetics.
  • Authentic Feedback: Implementing advanced sarcasm detection to accurately correct and interpret review polarities.

Methodology Pipeline

1

Data Foundation and Preprocessing

The framework draws from SLTDA reports, Google Trends, TripAdvisor, and Booking.com. Monthly arrival figures are down-sampled into daily predictions via PyMC Bayesian modeling. Features include rainfall log-transformation and Bayesian smooth target encoding for holidays. Consensus Feature Selection utilizes Pearson, Lasso, SHAP, and Random Forest Importance.

2

Integrated Modeling Layers

  • Arrival Forecasting: Employs a hybrid ensemble of SVR and TSFormer optimized via Bayesian Optimization (Test R² of 0.9230).
  • Flow Distribution: Uses a Global Panel Matrix to project visitor flows across 15 sites (Panel Ridge Regression reached 89.26% accuracy).
  • Recommendation & Sentiment: A full hybrid model integrating XLM-RoBERTa and VGG visual features, alongside BERT-based sarcasm detection (F1: 0.8896) to correct review polarities.
