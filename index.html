Digital Tourism Intelligence Framework

A unified AI architecture designed to transition Sri Lanka's tourism industry from "intuition-first" planning to data-driven precision.

Explore The Research Meet the Team

Project Abstract

This research introduces the Digital Tourism Intelligence Framework (DTIF), a revolutionary approach to destination management. Despite recording 2.36 million arrivals in 2025, Sri Lanka's tourism strategies remain heavily reliant on guesswork.

Critical management components such as arrival forecasting, dynamic flow distribution, and real-time sentiment analysis are treated as isolated tasks. This leads to unmanaged congestion at major sites like Sigiriya and diminished traveler satisfaction. The DTIF architecture synchronizes the needs of both authorities and travelers through a single, shared multimodal AI foundation.

Integrated Modeling Layers

📈

Predictive Forecasting

Employs a high-reliability hybrid ensemble of SVR and TSFormer to accurately capture non-linear, shock-sensitive national tourism demand signals.

🗺️

Flow Management

Projects visitor flows across 15 sites using a Global Panel Matrix, calculating a dynamic Visitor Load Index (VLI) to mitigate hotspot congestion.

🧠

Experiential Intelligence

Delivers multilingual, multimodal recommendations utilizing advanced BERT-based sarcasm detection to correctly interpret authentic review polarities.

Dual Master-Facing Channels

📊 Tourism Intelligence Dashboard

A comprehensive web application designed specifically for the SLTDA and stakeholders. It focuses on strategic arrival forecasting and site-level congestion management to ensure sustainable capacity limits.

📱 TripSense Mobile App

A specialized traveler-facing application that draws from our multimodal data pipeline to provide highly personalized accommodation recommendations and deep, culturally-aware destination insights.
